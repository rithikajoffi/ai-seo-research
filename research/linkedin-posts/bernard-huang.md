Post 2 — May 2026
For a long time, one of the first questions people asked about AI models was their knowledge cutoff. But from what we've been seeing in testing at Clearscope, that question is becoming less relevant.
Not because models suddenly "know everything," but because they're increasingly looking things up before answering. In many cases, what shows up in an answer isn't coming from training alone — it's coming from what the system retrieves in real time.
And that changes the dynamic quite a bit.
It means new information can influence answers much faster than people expect. It also means visibility isn't gated by the next model release anymore. Instead, it's shaped by whether your content is part of the set the model chooses to read.
We've been seeing pretty consistent patterns here, especially in how different systems approach retrieval vs. recall. What's going on: knowledge cutoffs are starting to matter less than retrieval eligibility.
