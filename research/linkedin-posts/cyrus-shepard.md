## Post 2 — May 2026

By now, everyone understands that Google + AI Answers are brutal to the traditional SEO playbook of "informational content."

- The Skyscraper Technique doesn't work as well if Google Gemini can create a better page than you instantly, on any topic. Links won't save you either.
- Currently, only ~40% of Google searches end with a click to third-party websites.
- A huge portion of those Google clicks are branded/navigational searches to specific sites (e.g., Reddit, Facebook, YouTube, etc.)

So how can publishers shift their content strategies to increase their chances of capturing the remaining clicks, show up in AI citations, and improve brand visibility?

Publishers are going to need either: 1) A strong brand, or 2) First-party information — information that you can't get directly from AI. That information can take several forms: original data, first-person perspectives, UGC communities, etc.

I looked at hundreds of sites currently winning Google, then classified all these different content types and rated them on their value and chance of success in a Google Zero world.
