Post 1 — June 2026
Before I release a new Claude Code skill to AI Accelerator members, I have AI pretend to be those members and beta test it for me first.
Different personas. Different businesses. Different ways of misusing the prompt. All simulated by Claude before any real member touches it.
AI tests AI for me.
The fake members catch issues faster than I would. They actually try the weird inputs I'd skip out of laziness. Broken instructions, missing edge cases, voice drift.
All caught before a real member files a single bug report.

Post 2 — May 2026
I can run 4 AI agents at once. I can't do it for more than 6 hours.
Everyone's selling you "spin up 10 agents in parallel, 10x your output."
The reality is quieter: your brain is the bottleneck, not the tooling.
After 6 hours of context-switching across 3-4 simultaneous sessions, my brain just gives up. Not tired in a "I need coffee" way. More like I can't hold the threads anymore.
Ken, a member on a recent mastermind call, nailed it: cognitive decline and cognitive confusion. That's what it actually feels like.
And I think that's why the new Claude Code Desktop deliberately shipped a simpler interface than VS Code. Less visual clutter, fewer panels, fewer things competing for attention. They traded some features for less cognitive load. I'd make the same call.
So if you're scaling AI across your team, maybe the better question isn't "how many agents can we run" — it's how do we protect the human in the loop.
