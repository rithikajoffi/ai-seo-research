Post 1 — June 2026
AI answers are probabilistic, but prompt tracking can still become more accurate.
The backlash against prompt tracking gets one thing right: one prompt run is not enough. 5 people can run the same prompt and get 5 different answers.
The wrong conclusion is that prompt tracking is useless. The better conclusion is that prompt tracking needs better sampling.
Run prompts multiple times. Keep platform scores separate. Add confidence intervals. Track personas and follow-up journeys. Store raw answers so you can audit whether the issue is the model, the scorer, the scraper, or the prompt.
Prompt tracking will not become keyword tracking. It will look more like polling.

Post 2 — May 2026
Even Anthropic is still figuring out marketing in the AI era.
5 takeaways from AirOps Next in NYC:
Nobody has the magic sauce. The leading model developers are planning 2-3 months ahead. That's the planning horizon everyone is working with now.
The room was a real mix of C-suite execs and practitioners. Both groups are looking for answers.
The future is agentic. AirOps launched Quill, and the conversation kept coming back to agents automating work. Headcount didn't come up once. Everybody wants productivity output to go up.
Brand finally gets the spotlight it always deserved. AI marketing leans heavier on brand, audience, and trust than SEO ever did. Less of the performance-marketing flavor that defined the last decade.
AI-native companies and companies bolting AI onto an existing org operate on different planets. The gap is wider than I expected.
