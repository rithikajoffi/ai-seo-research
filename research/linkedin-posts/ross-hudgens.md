Post 1 — June 2026
What's the current state of SEO careers?
I chatted about this in-depth with Eli Schwartz in our latest podcast. The takeaways were varied in up and down ways for practitioners.
First, Eli believes demand for SEO will be up as the dust settles. Things are getting more complex, and there are fewer people to do the work. This will be clearer if we do get some kind of LLM data that can make attribution easier for executives.
Second, the transition our teams are going through is that SEO teams are getting smaller, but how we do SEO/GEO is transitioning to touching other departments. This means vertical promotions in-track are getting less likely. However, the horizontal ways GEO is touching other channels should mean opportunities for more generalist promotions (e.g. Director of Marketing) that were previously harder to come by.
Finally, there has been an interesting inversion in technical demand. When we talked about careers several years ago, technical was declining as CMSs got smarter. With LLM complexity and agent-readiness, that got turned on its head.
That said, the counterbalance is that the "new" SEO/GEO is cross-functional. Being a heads-down technical practitioner is increasingly challenged in its ability to drive organizational impact.

Post 2 — May 2026
Most teams build "A vs B" pages. The more interesting play is A vs B vs C.
In tightly clustered markets, three-way comparison pages address a unique use case that two-way pages miss.
Ahrefs vs Semrush vs Moz
Vanta vs Drata vs Secureframe
Salesforce vs HubSpot vs Pipedrive
The test: if your category has a real top three, where everyone in the buying conversation already knows all three names, you have a defensible page worth writing.
The reason is that the trifecta comparison is a real method of buyer evaluation, and that can be verified by looking for search volume associated with it. The numbers will generally be low, but they will be capable of driving significant pipeline.
This format also maps nicely to the more complex searches users will do on LLMs. By having the transactional content to meet it, you give yourself a better chance to win.
One word of caution: we risk cannibalization with this approach. Evaluate every search to determine if the user intent truly communicates that pages like this are needed.
